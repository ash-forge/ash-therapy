# Crisis Resources

**If you're in immediate danger, please call emergency services (911 in US) or go to your nearest emergency room.**

These resources are embedded in ash-therapy and work without internet connection.

## How Ash-Therapy Uses This Information

### Embedded Resources
These crisis numbers are **embedded in the ash-therapy model** and available **without internet connection**.

### Location-Based Display
If you provide your location during setup, ash-therapy shows relevant resources first:
```
User location: United States
  → Shows 988, Crisis Text Line, etc.

User location: United Kingdom  
  → Shows Samaritans, Shout, etc.
```

### Crisis Detection
When ash-therapy detects crisis language, it immediately shows:
1. Your local crisis hotline
2. Alternative resources
3. Emergency services number
4. Option to call directly (if network enabled)

### No Internet Required
All resources are stored locally. You can access them:
- In airplane mode
- Without network connection
- In areas with poor connectivity

## Important Notes

### Confidentiality
- All hotlines listed are **confidential**
- They won't tell your parents, school, or employer (with rare exceptions for immediate danger)
- You don't have to give your real name

### Free Services
- Most hotlines are **free to call/text**
- Some may show up on phone bills (ask the hotline for alternatives)

### Language Support
- Many hotlines have multilingual support
- Ask if your language is available

### What to Expect
- Trained counselors (not AI)
- Non-judgmental listening
- Help with safety planning
- Referrals to local resources
- Follow-up support (if you want)
